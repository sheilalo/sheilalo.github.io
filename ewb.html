EWB UK: Solar Water Pump September 2015 - November 2015

Overview

As part of MECE09020: Mechanical Design Project 3, students participated in the Engineers Without Borders (EWB) UK design challenges that aimed to develop solutions for the community in Bambui, Cameroon. My group chose to design a solar water pump system to address water shortages during dry season.

Process

First, we researched how the current filtered water supply system operated. Right now, the gravity-fed system diverts water into a filtration process before it reaches the taps in the community. Then, we estimated the population growth rate in Cameroon, average water use per person, and average daily sunlight duration to begin sizing our system. This was done in conjunction with examining the limited data we had on the regionâ€™s topography in order to determine where to place the storage water tanks.

Final Design

Final solar water pump design

In the end, we decided to design a system that complemented the current water supply system by adding another storage tank at lower altitudes that would feed into an existing tank at higher altitude. Water would be routed through a similar stream intake system that led to a low altitude storage tank. A submersible centrifugal pump powered by solar panels would then pump the water to a higher altitude strange tank, which would feed into the current filtration system.
